Structure of my blog posts

I was overhearing the CTO of Basecamp over a podcast called Rework. He shared the way he used to write blog posts.

  • He took 3-4 minutes to write 4 paragraphs of text about the topic which he has validated.
  • He used twitter to validate his idea. If a post invited lot of replies or retweets. Then thats a good topic to write a post on.

Coming back to my blog

  • I cannot write a blog post in 3 minutes because I don’t have anyone on my team to prep it up or improve it.
  • I have to go with my enthusiasm and prediction before I post it out because I am starting out on twitter.
  • My friends and family believes that I am wasting time writing a blog :D

Structure of my blog

Top Three Categories

  • Technology (tech, libraries & frameworks used at work)
  • Blog (backstage/blog related stories)
  • Books (book talks)

Type of posts

  • Sharing how I customized or used tech at work
  • Sharing book recommendations
  • Sharing stories related to my blog because growing a blog is not intuitive or something that builds naturally like fat in my body :D

I don’t get much time to decorate the blog post with pictures or spice it up with keywords.

Why I paused blog related analytics

  • For the first 3 months, the blog analytics will be turned off.
  • don’t take Google SEO too seriously.

  • I did not enable comments.

    I want to do the above 2 steps because I don’t want to be distracted. The purpose of my blog is

  • I want to help people who are travelling my road

  • I want to get feedback and guidance from experts.
  • I want others to know what I do.

The future of my blog

I have a road map. I believe in roadmap and sticking on to it. More about it on this post

Resources that I share

  • Not much code. I don’t believe in spoon feeding developers. Let them figure it out. There is no get smart quick in development.

  • I share links to websites towards the end of the blog post that were mentioned or referenced.
