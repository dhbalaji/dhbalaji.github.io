How I Increased Home Wifi Router Security without spending a buck

I am a fan of securing things. Partly as a minimalist I own or get attached to very few things. I value the things and use them to the fullest.

Home router is one device which I use almost everyday. My ISP serves internet through cable and I have to take care of the wireless infrastructure myself.

I use Dlink router 600M. Its a pretty basic model available around 1000 Rupees in India.

Here are the 9 things that I changed

  • Change the URL on which you access the settings for the wireless router settings. By default, all Dlink routers page can be accessed at 192.168.0.1. Change it to something else.

  • Change the password for the user. By defualt, its Admin, you can set a secure password.

  • Disable WPS setup

  • Disable Broadcast SSID. No one can see your wifi access point name when they are scanning for signals. Joining to wifi is simple, you need to know the exact SSID name and password. You can tell your foes who are dumb that your router is not working :)

  • Restrict connected devices by MACaddress. Only listed devices can access your router.

  • Enable Wireless Isolation

  • Have a strong Pre-Shared Key. This is the key to encrypt/decrypt data.

  • Disable UPnP

  • If you know any sites which have a security risk, block then using URL Blocking Capability

  • Download the configuration file and back it up. This will help you get your config back when someone resets your router. Time to back up is less than configuring it.

  • Change the DNS to google DNS. i.e. 8.8.8.8 & 4.4.4.4

Caveats

  • Anyone who has physical access to the device and can reset it. You will lose all your settings.

  • Its a pain to connect devices to hidden SSID & manually adding mac id to the network.

  • If your wifi network is having 10+ clients, better avoid this setup. Connecting chromecast and that 10 year old smart phone to internet is a pain. You need to figure out their mac address.

  • My router did not have Guest mode. This is something we should look for.

Tips

  • I do not connect mobiles to home wifi network. They kill my family time.

  • Avoid mobile data on all devices, I learnt to share internet over mobile hotspot whenever required.
