Experience attending Google Developer Day 2017 in Bangalore

Hi guys, I am delighted to have attended the Google Developer Day 2017 in Bangalore.

Official site - Google Developer Day

Flash back

Sometimes back, I got email about Google Developer Day and asking to express interest in a Google form. I remember entering my Name, profession etc. I was notified that I was on waitlist over another email. Few days later, I got another email informing that I was confirmed.

Few weeks before developer day

I got an another email informing the

  • Agenda
  • Venue
  • Featured sessions
  • Getting to the venue

A few more emails followed, they informed me about

  • Review clinics
  • Instructor led trainings
  • Codelabs
  • Cloud certification exam room
  • Ola offer for commute
  • Makemytrip offer for stay
  • few more instructions about required software for code labs etc

I got my Confirmation code which is like the VISA for the event. Code + Govt ID card was needed.

Packing my bag

I carried my laptop with charger along with few more stuff like notes, tablet, books, headphone etc. Most of them were unnecessary. They just added weight to my backpack.

It was raining so I had to stuff my rain coat, thermals etc.

Most importantly I had to install the software listed in the email. This step is needed to attend code labs.

I painstakingly shortlisted the courses listed in the agenda. Basically there are three types of programs or sessions you can expect. They are

  • Sessions - some of them will be live streamed. No need of laptop.
  • Codelabs
  • Training

At the Venue

I hurried to the venue but reached exactly at 9.55am. Event was supposed to start at 10am. I was standing in the long line at the registration desk. There were 5 counters to help the developers. Just show the govt photo ID card or aadhard and your confirmation code.

To my surprise the folks along with me in the line were flipkart employees. Most people were wearing their company swag. As expected I was also given GDG T Shirt

I swiftly moved to the hall where the keynote was held. Sounds from the speakers & flawless big screens made me forget my hustle getting to the venue & queue at the registration desk.

Most of the event videos can be found at the official site, link mentioned above.

Fast forward

  • Event started at 10.15am.
  • Keynote speech is a mashup of what to expect. Its given by bunch of people who are experts in that domain.
  • The live streamed events are formal events, some events are very casual ones.
  • Wifi did not work, rely on your own internet if needed.
  • There is a special place for
    • Product demos
    • Stalls
    • Community lounge
    • Code labs
  • Certification room where you can take up Google cerification for a discounted price
  • Superb breakfast, lunch and snacks
  • You get to meet
    • Google staff
    • Google developer experts
    • Other googlers from UK, USA etc
  • Walking around and finding venue for each session is bit hard.
  • Sometimes you have stand in long line for goodies, you may have to stand in line for sessions too.
  • Meeting and talking to experts give you credible source of information
  • You get special links for tutorials etc. I got one from pluralsight.

Things that were discussed

  • Progressive Web Apps
  • Android & Android things
  • Firebase
  • Tensor flow & tensor flow lite
  • AMP
  • Performance stuff
  • Design thinking workshop

Recordings & Photos

Visit official site - Google Developer Day

Downside

  • Event coordinators were not so responsive
  • Transport back from event was miserable. I mean the Route A & B. They sped away at 6.30pm while we were playing big web quiz with Jake.

Verdict

  • Worth attending, plan your events
  • Have your queries handy, lot of googlers and experts around
  • Codelabs, Review & Certification room needs lot of home work to be done before hand.

Thanks Google.
