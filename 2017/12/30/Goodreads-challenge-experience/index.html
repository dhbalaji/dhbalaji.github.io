How I set and hit the Goodreads Challenge in 2016, 2017

Background

For those who are not aware of Goodreads Challenge, here is the short introduction.

Goodreads is a social networking site for readers. Users review, catalog and showcase their reads on Goodreads.

Reading Challenge is a feature in Goodreads which lets you pledge the number of books you want to read in a year. It could be any number of books.

How Goodreads challenge works

  • User pledges to read N number of books in a year. N can be any number based on your reading prowess.

  • Once the reader finishes reading a book, he needs to update the status on Goodreads.

  • The user should set the finish date to some date within the year. Without valid finish date, the book won’t be counted as part of your challenge.

  • You can see the status anytime, you can compare it with your friends and stay accountable.

Benefits of the challenge

  • You take reading as a sport.

  • Social pressure for the good. Your friends are watching your progress so you tend to stay alert and focused.

  • Helps to challenge yourself to read faster. You will learn speed reading

About my challenge

2016

Target: 90 books

Achieved: 93 books

Lessons:

  • The goal was tough to keep up.
  • Need to exert on it. Forgot to get the chores done.
  • It was an enjoyable and proud experience to read many books in a year.
  • Had to read at least one book in 4 days throughout the year.

2017

Target: 30 books

Achieved: 40 books

Lessons:

  • A comfortable goal.
  • Needed no exertion.
  • I would set a higher number next time.

2018

Target: 48 books

  • The books to be read have been shortlisted. I never did this earlier. Kind of excited.
  • All the books were purchased by me in the past but never read till date. Its kind of complete reading what you buy.
  • Need to read at least one book a week throughout the year.

Tips

  • Set a goal which exerts you.
  • Set a challenge and let your friends know about it. That’s a way you can stick on to it.
  • Don’t forget to update the reading status from time to time.
  • Mix light reads and heavy reads to meet the goal. Of course, no one can read 90 heavy reads in a year while keeping up his day job.

Is it worth it?

Is reading challenge worth it? YES!

  • No fees attached, its free.
  • You stay focused.
  • Gives a reason to catalog reads.

Downsides

  • Can be stressful if the goal needs exertion.
  • Can be shameful if you don’t hit the target.
  • Another chore like updating reads, write review etc. Of course, these are good chores compared to workplace chores.

Wish you all the best with your reading challenge!
