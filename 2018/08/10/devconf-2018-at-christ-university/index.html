Attending DevConf 2018 at Christ University

I was back at the university after 8 long years. This time at the Christ University Bangalore to attend DevConf.in, the second meeting from redhat.

The registrations happened on eventbrite website. I am surprised at the number of technical events and meetups happening at Bangalore. Every weekend we have atleast 2 interesting meetups happening. These meetups are enablers of good developer community.

Coming back to devconf, it was backed by Redhat. They are definitely not the goto people for seeking frontend knowledge. There were quite an interesting lineup of workshops on containers, devops and React native. Yeah, react native was the goto workshop for me.

Conference starts

The team from Christ university performed classical dance in a huge theater. The dance was amazing, the message they conveyed through dance was profound. The light patterns were captivating everyone attention. I am sure, the foreigners would have appreciated our Indian dance forms.

Keynote speech

The keynote was centered around “Open Source”. Here are couple of bullet points about the speech.

  • Start of open source
  • Why open source
  • Open source does not mean free
  • Importance of licensing
  • Using multiple open source licenses along side each other
  • Open source and careers
  • Advantages of open source

Then we breaked for the events happening at multiple places within the campus.

Booths / Stalls

Though the number of stalls were few, they were related to open source in one way or the other

  • Red hat careers
  • Mozilla
  • Karanataka open source fed
  • Freeman
  • Devopedia
  • Fedora and centOS
  • Women who code

Picked some amazing stickers and no goodies. Got links to mozila A frame and VR projects. Planning to build the future of online bookshopping with web VR.

Sessions

  • Pattern framework introduction
    • Was actually pitching for contributors
    • Was less technical
  • Design system with react
    • Convert designs to real world react components
  • Modern web authentication with Auth0
    • Speech was centered around modern features in Auth0

There were a couple more on PWA etc.. I had to leave early.

Overall a good conference and good line up of speakers.
