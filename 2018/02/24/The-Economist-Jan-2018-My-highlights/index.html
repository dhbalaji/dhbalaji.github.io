The Economist Jan 2018 magazine - my highlights

I have a profound interest in economics because that shows how financially efficient an entity is. It could be a person, a family or a country.

The economist is a magazine printed in the USA. The interesting highlight was that, they covered almost all popular developing countries.

The cover of the magazine had Donald Trump with his one year success as a president.

Here are the three things which I had highlighted when reading this magazine.

Top 1: The missing Indian middle class

This article caught my attention because I live in India and I used to think I am middle class person. The heading and the figure shows that there is no middle class in India.

Interesting read, I am convinced that there is no middle class in India. Even if there were one, they are making good enough to feed the family but not enough to spend on starbucks coffee everyday. The comparison with Chinese middle class throws some interesting high lights.

Top 2: The Indian Aadhar, uniquely vulnerable.

This is again a controversial topic to me. First they gave use a 12 digit number, then they are trying to mask it. Someone can change our biometric information in the database and we Indians might not be able to access govt. services etc. Yes Its uniquely vulnerable.

The article shows some common issues like private apps interference and data sold for $8 etc.

Top 3: Very minimal ads

Yeah economists cannot be fooled by ads like common consumers.
